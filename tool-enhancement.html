This interactive tattoo age calculator uses up-to-date state statutes and regulations to provide accurate, instant results about tattoo age eligibility.

The tool analyzes four key factors to determine eligibility:

  • Your State: Each of the 50 US states has unique tattoo age laws. Some states require you to be 18 with no exceptions, while others allow minors aged 16-17 to get tattoos with proper parental authorization.
  • Your Current Age: The minimum legal age for tattoos varies by state. Most states require 18 years old, but states like Florida, Arkansas, and Colorado allow tattoos at 16 with parental consent.
  • Parental Consent: In states that permit minor tattoos, written parental or guardian consent is typically required. Some states also require consent to be notarized.
  • Parental Presence: Many states mandate that a parent or legal guardian be physically present during the entire tattooing procedure for minors, not just for signing consent forms.

Frequently Asked Questions About the Tattoo Age Checker

Is the tattoo age checker accurate?

Yes, our tattoo age checker is highly accurate and based on current state statutes and regulations. We regularly update our database to reflect changes in state tattoo laws. However, we recommend verifying results with your state health department or local tattoo parlor, as local ordinances may impose additional restrictions beyond state law.

Do you store my age or state information?

No, we do not store any personal information you enter into the tattoo age checker. All calculations are performed locally in your browser using JavaScript. We do not collect, save, or transmit your age, state, or any other information you input into the tool. Your privacy is completely protected.

Can this tool be used for piercing age laws?

This specific tool is designed for tattoo age laws only. However, we have a comprehensive Piercing Age Laws section covering ear piercing, body piercing, and genital piercing laws for all 50 states. Piercing laws often differ from tattoo laws, with many states allowing ear piercings at younger ages with parental consent.

Does the legal tattoo age change often?

Tattoo age laws are relatively stable but can change. State legislatures occasionally amend tattoo statutes to make laws more or less restrictive. We monitor all 50 state legislatures for proposed and enacted legislation affecting tattoo age requirements and update our tool within 30 days of any law changes. Major changes are also announced on our blog.

What should I do if the tool says I'm not eligible?

If the age checker indicates you're not currently eligible for a tattoo in your state, the tool will tell you exactly how many years you need to wait. In the meantime, consider these alternatives: temporary tattoos, henna designs, or waiting until you reach the legal age. Some states also allow medical tattoos for reconstructive purposes with physician approval, even for minors.

Can I get a tattoo in a different state if I'm not eligible in my home state?

Technically, tattoo age laws are based on where the tattoo is performed, not your state of residence. However, crossing state lines specifically to evade your home state's tattoo age restrictions may have legal consequences. Additionally, reputable tattoo parlors typically require proof of residency or refuse to tattoo out-of-state minors. Consult with a family law attorney before pursuing this option.

What happens if a tattoo artist tattoos me illegally?

If a tattoo artist tattoos a minor in violation of state law, they face serious consequences including: fines ranging from $500 to $10,000, license suspension or revocation, criminal charges (misdemeanor or felony), and civil liability. The minor and parents may also face legal consequences in some states. Never attempt to get a tattoo illegally, as it puts both you and the artist at risk.
