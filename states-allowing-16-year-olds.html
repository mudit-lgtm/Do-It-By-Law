States Allowing 16-Year-Olds to Get Tattoos 2026

Complete guide to tattoo requirements for ages 16-17

📅 Updated: January 13, 2026 📋 15 States Allow Ages 16-17

Can a 16-Year-Old Get a Tattoo?

Yes, in 15 US states, 16-year-olds can legally receive tattoos with proper parental consent and documentation.

  • ✓ 15 states allow tattoos for ages 16-17
  • ✓ Written parental consent required in all cases
  • ✓ Some states require notarization
  • ⚠️ Parent must be present during procedure in most states
  • ⚠️ Many studios refuse minors despite legal permission

Documentation Requirements for 16-17 Year Olds

Required Documents (All States)

  1. Minor's Valid Photo ID
    • State-issued driver's license or learner's permit
    • State identification card
    • U.S. passport
  2. Parent/Guardian Valid Photo ID
    • Must be government-issued
    • Must be current (not expired)
  3. Proof of Relationship
    • Birth certificate showing parent-child relationship
    • Adoption papers (if applicable)
    • Court-ordered guardianship documents
  4. Written Consent Form
    • Signed in presence of tattoo artist
    • Notarized (Florida, New Jersey)
    • Includes acknowledgment of permanence

Common Questions for 16-17 Year Olds

Can I get a tattoo at 16 without my parents knowing?

No. All states that allow 16-year-olds to get tattoos require explicit parental consent. Attempting to forge consent or use fake identification is illegal and carries criminal penalties.

Will most tattoo studios actually tattoo a 16-year-old?

Even in states where it's legal, many professional studios refuse to tattoo anyone under 18 due to liability concerns, insurance requirements, and professional ethics. Call ahead to confirm studio policies.

What if I'm 16 and emancipated?

Emancipation status varies by state. Some states allow emancipated 16-17 year olds to get tattoos without parental consent if they provide court-ordered emancipation papers. Check your specific state's laws.

Can my guardian (not parent) give consent?

Yes, legal guardians can provide consent if they have court-ordered guardianship documents. You'll need to bring proof of guardianship in addition to standard identification requirements.

Important Warnings for 16-17 Year Olds

⚠️ Think Before You Ink

Statistics show that individuals who get tattoos as minors are:

  • 3x more likely to seek tattoo removal
  • More likely to experience tattoo regret
  • May face employment discrimination
  • Risk complications if tattoo artist is unlicensed

Consider These Questions:

  • Will you still want this design in 5, 10, 20 years?
  • How will this affect college applications or job interviews?
  • Can you afford professional laser removal ($200-$500 per session, 6-10 sessions typical)?
  • Have you researched the tattoo artist's portfolio and licensing?

Alternatives to Consider

If you're 16-17 and considering a tattoo, try these alternatives first:

  • Wait Until 18: Only 2 years away, and you'll have full legal control
  • Temporary Tattoos: Test drive your design with henna or airbrush
  • Custom Artwork: Commission a drawing of your design to display
  • Save Money: Use waiting period to save for a high-quality artist
  • Refine Design: Give yourself time to ensure you love the design
