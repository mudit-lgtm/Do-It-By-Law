Most Lenient Tattoo Age Laws by State 2026

States allowing minors ages 14-17 to get tattoos with parental consent

📅 Updated: January 13, 2026 🟢 23 States Allow Minors with Consent

Quick Summary

23 US states allow minors as young as 14-17 to receive tattoos with proper parental consent and presence.

  • ✓ Ages 14-17 may qualify with parental consent
  • ✓ Written consent required (some require notarization)
  • ✓ Parent/guardian must often be present
  • ⚠️ Individual studios may still refuse minors

States Allowing Minors with Parental Consent

Ages 14-15 Allowed (Most Lenient)

Min Age: 14 with consent

Min Age: 14 with consent

Statute: Nev. Rev. Stat. § 597.850

Ages 16-17 Allowed

Florida

Min Age: 16 with notarized consent

Statute: Fla. Stat. § 381.0075

New Jersey

Min Age: 16 with notarized consent

Statute: N.J. Rev. Stat. § 2C:40-21

Vermont

Min Age: 16 with consent

Statute: Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 26 § 4101

Parental Consent Requirements

States allowing minors to get tattoos typically require:

  • Written parental consent signed in presence of tattoo artist
  • Valid government-issued ID for both parent and minor
  • Proof of parent-child relationship (birth certificate)
  • Parent present during the tattooing procedure (varies by state)
  • Notarization of consent form (Florida, New Jersey)

Important Considerations

Studio Policies May Be Stricter

Even in lenient states, many reputable studios refuse to tattoo anyone under 18 due to:

  • Liability insurance requirements
  • Professional ethical standards
  • High regret rates among minors
  • Potential for parental disputes

Documentation Checklist

Before scheduling an appointment, ensure you have:

  1. Minor's government-issued photo ID or birth certificate
  2. Parent/guardian government-issued photo ID
  3. Birth certificate proving parent-child relationship
  4. Notarized consent form (if required by state)
  5. Confirmation of studio's willingness to tattoo minors
