Alabama Piercing Age Laws

Complete guide to ear and body piercing requirements in Alabama

Quick Summary

👂 Ear Piercing:

Any age w/ consent

💍 Body Piercing:

18+ only

🔞 Genital Piercing:

Prohibited

Detailed Requirements

Ear Piercing Requirements

Minimum Age: Any age w/ consent

Ear lobe piercing is generally the most permissive type of piercing in Alabama. Most establishments allow ear piercing at any age as long as a parent or legal guardian is present and provides consent.

Body Piercing Requirements

Minimum Age: 18+ only

Body piercings (including nose, eyebrow, navel, tongue) have stricter requirements. In Alabama, you must be 18 or older to get a body piercing, with no exceptions for parental consent.

Genital Piercing

Status: Prohibited

Alabama prohibits genital piercing on anyone under 18 years old, regardless of parental consent. This includes nipple piercings in most cases.

⚠️ Important Restrictions

Parent must be present for minors

Always verify specific requirements with your chosen piercing studio, as individual establishments may have additional policies.

Parental Consent Requirements

When parental consent is required in Alabama, you typically need:

  • Valid government-issued ID for both minor and parent
  • Proof of guardianship (birth certificate, court documents, etc.)
  • Parent/guardian must be physically present during the procedure
  • Written consent form signed by parent/guardian

Safety & Health Considerations

Before getting a piercing in Alabama, ensure:

  • The piercing studio is properly licensed and regulated
  • All equipment is sterile and single-use
  • The piercer follows proper sanitation protocols
  • You understand aftercare instructions
  • You're aware of potential risks and complications
