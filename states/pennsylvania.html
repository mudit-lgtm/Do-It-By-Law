Pennsylvania Tattoo Age Laws 2026

Quick Answer

Minimum legal age to get a tattoo in Pennsylvania: 18 years old

Parental consent for minors: ❌ Not allowed

Minimum Age to Get a Tattoo in Pennsylvania

In Pennsylvania, the minimum legal age to get a tattoo is 18 years old. This age requirement applies to all tattoo procedures, regardless of size, location on the body, or whether the tattoo is permanent or temporary.

Pennsylvania enforces a strict 18-year minimum age policy for tattoos. This means that anyone under 18 years old cannot legally receive a tattoo, even with parental consent or presence.

The only exceptions to Pennsylvania's tattoo age laws typically involve medical tattoos for reconstructive or therapeutic purposes, which may be performed on minors with physician approval and parental consent. These medical exceptions are evaluated on a case-by-case basis.

Parental Consent Rules in Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania does not allow minors under 18 to get tattoos, even with parental consent. This means that written parental authorization, parental presence, and notarized consent forms are all legally irrelevant for tattoo age requirements in Pennsylvania.

The state's strict 18-year minimum age policy reflects Pennsylvania's position that tattoos are a permanent body modification decision that should only be made by legal adults. Many legislators and health officials in Pennsylvania believe that minors, even with parental approval, may not fully understand the long-term implications and health risks of tattoos.

If you're under 18 and living in Pennsylvania, your options include:

  • Waiting until you turn 18 years old
  • Considering temporary alternatives like henna tattoos or temporary tattoo transfers
  • Researching tattoo designs and artists so you're fully prepared when you reach legal age

Penalties for Violating Tattoo Age Laws in Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania takes violations of tattoo age laws seriously, imposing significant penalties on tattoo artists, shop owners, and establishments that tattoo minors illegally.

Penalties for tattoo artists and shops include:

  • Criminal charges: Violating Pennsylvania's tattoo age laws can result in misdemeanor or felony charges, depending on circumstances and repeat offenses.
  • Fines: Monetary penalties typically range from $500 to $10,000 per violation, with higher fines for repeat offenders or aggravating circumstances.
  • License suspension: The Pennsylvania Department of Health or equivalent licensing authority can suspend tattoo artist licenses for violations, ranging from 30 days to permanent revocation.
  • License revocation: Serious or repeated violations can result in permanent loss of tattooing privileges in Pennsylvania.
  • Civil liability: Artists and shops may face civil lawsuits from parents or guardians for damages, medical costs, and emotional distress.

Liability for minors and parents:

While Pennsylvania law primarily holds tattoo artists and establishments liable, minors who obtain tattoos illegally may face:

  • Difficulty obtaining future tattoos from reputable establishments
  • Potential complications with school policies or employment in certain fields
  • Health risks from unlicensed, underground tattoo operations

Frequently Asked Questions About Pennsylvania Tattoo Laws

Can a 16 year old get a tattoo in Pennsylvania?

No, Pennsylvania requires individuals to be at least 18 years old to get a tattoo. No parental consent exceptions apply for 16-year-olds.

Does Pennsylvania allow tattooing minors with parental consent?

No, Pennsylvania does not allow tattooing of minors under 18, even with parental consent. The state enforces a strict 18-year minimum age policy with no exceptions.

Can I get a tattoo at 17 in Pennsylvania?

No, you must be 18 years old to get a tattoo in Pennsylvania. No exceptions for parental consent exist for 17-year-olds.

What happens if a tattoo artist ignores Pennsylvania age laws?

Tattoo artists who violate Pennsylvania age laws face serious consequences including fines ($500-$10,000), license suspension or revocation, criminal charges (misdemeanor or felony), and civil liability. The Pennsylvania Department of Health actively investigates complaints and conducts inspections to ensure compliance.

Are there medical exceptions for minors in Pennsylvania?

Yes, Pennsylvania typically allows medical tattoos for reconstructive or therapeutic purposes on minors with physician approval and parental consent. These procedures include nipple reconstruction after mastectomy, scar camouflage, and medical alert tattoos. Each case is evaluated individually.

Can I travel to another state to get a tattoo if I'm not old enough in Pennsylvania?

While tattoo laws are based on where the procedure is performed (not your residence), crossing state lines specifically to evade Pennsylvania's age restrictions may have legal and ethical implications. Many reputable tattoo parlors refuse to tattoo out-of-state minors. Consult with a family law attorney before pursuing this option.

What identification do I need to get a tattoo in Pennsylvania?

To get a tattoo in Pennsylvania, you must present valid government-issued photo identification such as a driver's license, state ID card, passport, or military ID. The ID must show your date of birth and photo. Tattoo parlors are required to verify and record this information.
