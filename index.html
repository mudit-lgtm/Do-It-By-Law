Frequently Asked Questions

How old do you have to be to get a tattoo?

In most US states, you must be 18 years old to get a tattoo. Some states allow minors aged 16-17 to get tattooed with written parental consent and parental presence during the procedure.

Can minors get tattoos with parental consent?

It depends on the state. States like Alabama prohibit tattooing minors entirely, even with parental consent. Other states like Florida allow it at age 16 with notarized parental consent.

What happens if I lie about my age?

Providing false information about your age to get a tattoo is illegal in most states. You could face fines, and the tattoo artist could lose their license and face criminal charges.

Do I need parental presence or just consent?

This varies by state. Some states require only written consent, while others require the parent or legal guardian to be physically present during the tattoo procedure. Check your state's specific requirements.

Are piercing age laws the same as tattoo laws?

Not always. Some states have different age requirements for piercings versus tattoos. Ear piercings often have lower age limits than body tattoos.

Can I get a tattoo at 17 with parental consent?

It depends on your state. States like Colorado and Idaho allow this, while states like Alabama, Alaska, and California do not permit tattooing anyone under 18 regardless of parental consent.
