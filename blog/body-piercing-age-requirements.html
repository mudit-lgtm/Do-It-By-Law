Body Piercing Age Requirements: Complete State Guide 2026

December 14, 2026

Updated: December 8, 2026

Body piercings—including nose, eyebrow, navel, lip, and tongue piercings—are subject to stricter regulations than ear piercings. Most states have specific age requirements and parental consent rules for body piercings on minors.

Minimum Age Requirements by Piercing Type

Nose Piercings

Most states require minors to be at least 16 years old for nose piercings, even with parental consent. Some states set the minimum at 18 with no exceptions.

Navel (Belly Button) Piercings

Navel piercings typically require a minimum age of 16-18 years old. Many states prohibit navel piercings for anyone under 18, regardless of parental consent.

Lip and Tongue Piercings

Oral piercings are heavily regulated due to health risks. Most states require individuals to be 18 years old with very few exceptions for minors with parental consent.

Eyebrow Piercings

Eyebrow piercings generally require a minimum age of 16 years old with notarized parental consent and parental presence.

Parental Consent Requirements

For states that allow body piercings on minors, requirements typically include:

  • Written and notarized parental consent
  • Parent/guardian must be present during entire procedure
  • Valid government-issued photo ID for both parent and minor
  • Proof of guardianship (birth certificate, custody papers)
  • Health history form completed by parent

States with Strict Body Piercing Laws

California

California prohibits most body piercings for minors under 18. Only ear piercings are permitted for minors with parental consent.

Georgia

Georgia requires individuals to be 18 years old for all body piercings except ear piercings. No parental consent exceptions.

Illinois

Illinois allows body piercings for minors 16+ with written parental consent and parental presence. Minors under 16 are restricted to ear piercings only.

Florida

Florida permits body piercings for minors 16+ with notarized parental consent. Parent must be present during procedure.

Texas

Texas requires minors to be 18 for most body piercings. Some studios may pierce minors 16-17 with extensive parental documentation.

Health and Safety Considerations

Risks of Body Piercings

  • Infection and bacterial complications
  • Allergic reactions to jewelry materials
  • Scarring and keloid formation
  • Nerve damage (especially oral piercings)
  • Rejection and migration of piercing
  • Blood-borne pathogen transmission (if equipment not sterile)

Choosing a Safe Piercing Studio

When selecting a body piercing studio:

  • Verify proper licensing and health department inspection
  • Ensure use of sterile, single-use needles (never piercing guns for body piercings)
  • Check that jewelry is implant-grade titanium or surgical steel
  • Confirm piercer has APP (Association of Professional Piercers) certification
  • Review aftercare instructions and follow-up protocols

Body Piercing vs. Ear Piercing Laws

Aspect Ear Piercing Body Piercing
Minimum Age 5-13 years (most states) 16-18 years (most states)
Parental Consent Written consent usually sufficient Notarized consent often required
Parental Presence Varies by state Usually required
Method Piercing gun often acceptable Sterile needle required

Medical Advice Before Body Piercings

Consult a healthcare provider before getting a body piercing if:

  • You have diabetes or immune system disorders
  • You're taking blood thinners or immunosuppressants
  • You have a history of keloid scarring
  • You have skin conditions like eczema or psoriasis
  • You have metal allergies

⚠️ Important Reminder

Body piercing laws are enforced to protect minors. Attempting to circumvent these laws by using fake IDs or lying about age can result in legal consequences for both the minor and the piercing studio.

Aftercare and Healing Times

Typical Healing Periods

  • Nose Piercing: 2-4 months
  • Navel Piercing: 6-12 months
  • Eyebrow Piercing: 2-3 months
  • Lip Piercing: 2-3 months
  • Tongue Piercing: 4-6 weeks

Essential Aftercare Steps

  • Clean piercing twice daily with saline solution
  • Avoid touching piercing with unwashed hands
  • Don't remove jewelry during healing period
  • Avoid swimming in pools, lakes, or oceans
  • Keep makeup and skincare products away from piercing
  • Watch for signs of infection (excessive redness, pus, fever)
