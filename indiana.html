Indiana Tattoo Age Laws 2025

Complete legal guide to tattoo age requirements in Indiana

📅 Updated: November 24, 2025 ⚖️ Indiana Code § 22-17A-2

Quick Answer

In Indiana, you must be 18 years old to get a tattoo. There are no exceptions for parental consent or parental presence.

  • ✓ Minimum age: 18 years old
  • ✗ Parental consent does NOT allow minors to get tattoos
  • ⚖️ Violation is a Class C misdemeanor
  • 📋 No documentation can override the age requirement

Indiana Legal Code

Indiana's tattoo age laws are governed by Indiana Code Title 22, Chapter 17A, Section 2 (IN Code § 22-17A-2).

IN Code § 22-17A-2 (2025)

"No person shall tattoo the body of any person under the age of 18 years. Any person who violates this section shall be guilty of a Class C misdemeanor."

This law is absolute and contains no exceptions for parental consent or medical necessity (excluding medical tattoos performed by licensed healthcare providers).

Penalties for Violations

For the Tattoo Artist

Charge: Class C Misdemeanor

Maximum Jail Time: Up to 3 months

Maximum Fine: Up to $500

License Impact: Suspension or revocation of tattoo artist license

For the Minor

While the law primarily targets tattoo artists, minors who provide false identification or misrepresent their age may face:

  • Criminal charges for fraud or forgery (if fake ID used)
  • Difficulty removing the tattoo (expensive and painful)
  • Social and professional consequences

Medical and Cultural Exceptions

Indiana law recognizes very limited exceptions to the tattoo age requirement:

✓ Medical Tattoos

Tattoos performed by licensed physicians, physician assistants, or nurses for medical purposes are exempt. This includes:

  • Radiation therapy marking
  • Reconstructive surgery marking
  • Medical identification tattoos

✗ NOT Valid Exceptions

The following do NOT exempt minors from the age requirement:

  • Religious or cultural ceremonies
  • Parental consent (written or in-person)
  • Emancipated minors
  • Married minors
  • Military service

Indiana Tattoo Laws FAQ

How old do you have to be to get a tattoo in Indiana?

You must be 18 years old to get a tattoo in Indiana. This is the minimum legal age with no exceptions for parental consent.

Can a 14 year old get a tattoo with parental consent in Indiana?

No. Indiana does not allow tattooing of anyone under 18, even with written parental consent and parental presence during the procedure.

Can you get a tattoo at 16 in Indiana?

No. The minimum age is 18 with absolutely no exceptions. You must wait until your 18th birthday to legally get a tattoo in Indiana.

What happens if you lie about your age to get a tattoo in Indiana?

Using false identification to get a tattoo is illegal. You could face charges for fraud, forgery, or identity misrepresentation. The tattoo artist would also face a Class C misdemeanor charge.

Are there any Indiana cities with different tattoo age laws?

No. Indiana state law applies uniformly across all cities and counties. Local jurisdictions cannot create more lenient age requirements than state law.

Can I get a piercing under 18 in Indiana?

Body piercing laws differ from tattoo laws. Indiana allows some piercings for minors with parental consent, but specific regulations vary by piercing type and location. Check Indiana piercing laws separately.
