Our Mission

Do It By Law was founded with a simple but important mission: to provide clear, accurate, and accessible information about tattoo, piercing, and body modification laws across the United States. We believe that everyone deserves to understand their legal rights and responsibilities when it comes to body art.

Whether you're a minor considering your first tattoo, a parent navigating consent requirements, or a professional artist ensuring compliance, we're here to help you navigate the complex landscape of body art legislation.

Our Commitment to Accuracy

We understand that legal information must be accurate and reliable. Our team continuously monitors:

  • State legislature websites for new bills and law changes
  • State health department regulations and updates
  • Professional licensing board requirements
  • Court decisions affecting body art regulations
  • Local ordinances and municipal codes

While we strive for accuracy, laws can change rapidly. We always recommend verifying current requirements with official sources before making decisions.

⚠️ Important Disclaimer

Do It By Law provides general legal information for educational purposes only. This website does not provide legal advice and should not be used as a substitute for consultation with licensed legal professionals. Laws vary by jurisdiction and can change frequently. Always verify current requirements with official state sources and consult with qualified attorneys for specific legal advice.

Our Editorial Standards

Research Process

  • All state laws verified against official state statutes
  • Primary sources: State health department regulations, administrative codes
  • Secondary sources: State bar associations, legal databases (Westlaw, LexisNexis)
  • Peer review by licensed attorneys before publication

Update Schedule

  • Quarterly review of all 50 state pages
  • Real-time monitoring of legislative changes via state legislature RSS feeds
  • Annual comprehensive audit of all content
  • User-submitted corrections reviewed within 48 hours

Accuracy Standards

  • Minimum two independent source verification for each state law
  • Direct citation of statute codes (e.g., AL Code § 22-1-17A)
  • Clear distinction between state law and studio policy
  • Medical claims reviewed by board-certified physicians

Transparency Policy

  • Last updated dates displayed on every page
  • Full citation list with clickable official sources
  • Editorial team credentials publicly available
  • Correction policy: Errors fixed within 24 hours with changelog
